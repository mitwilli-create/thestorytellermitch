The mechanics in one slide

What holds, what shifts

  • Holds: the film, the edit, the diegetic city ambience, and the brand voice. One cloned voice speaks every market; voice consistency is the brand asset.
  • Shifts: the language (transcreated by people, never machine-translated), the music bed (the score is cultural), and the match-night crowd shots, re-dressed in each market's colors where that market's rules allow it.
  • Signature: the end card keeps the Spanish tagline on screen in every market while the voice closes in the local language. A Spanish wine wears its origin.
Speaker notesThis is the workflow argument compressed. If they remember one sentence: market versions swap the voice track, the music bed, and the crowd's jerseys; everything else is reused. That reuse is where the cost delta on the receipts slide comes from.
One localization decision, up close

Transcreation is a creative act

"con la chispa justa" became "mit genau der richtigen Perlage"

Chispa is Spanish wine talk. A literal German translation would say Funke and sound ridiculous. Perlage is what a German wine copywriter actually writes. The template treats localized copy as an input, not something a machine guesses at; the tool does the production, their people keep the creative calls.

Speaker notesPull up the ES and DE scripts side by side if there is time; every market script in the kit carries per-line transcreation notes. This slide answers the "our markets hate translated ads" objection before it is raised: nothing here is auto-translated. The same discipline applies to picture: national colors are culture, federation crests are somebody's trademark, so the crowd wears plain kit.
The alcohol-category moment

Compliance variants are a re-edit, not a re-shoot

The set is compliance-tiered. France and Italy restrict alcohol-and-sport association, so their cuts are sport-free: neutral summer wardrobe, summer-evening copy, same film. France adds this product-only cut, the strict product-factual shape Loi Evin requires in the channels where French alcohol advertising is allowed at all (TV bans it outright): zero additional generation cost. Counsel reviews per market; the workflow makes their sign-off cheap to implement.

Speaker notesFor any alcohol brand in the room this is the slide that matters. Play the first seconds of the two French cuts side by side. Three tiers from one master: lifestyle with market jerseys where permitted, sport-free for France and Italy, product-only for France. The sentence this category has never heard: compliance variants are a re-edit, not a re-shoot. Never improvise specific market rules in the room; take the question away and bring counsel-reviewed language back.

Flows template A · reusable, workspace-shareable

Multi-market ad variant

Takes one master ad script plus transcreated copy per market and outputs a per-market voice track and market music bed, all in the same brand voice.

Most valuable for brand and agency production teams running one campaign across several European markets who currently re-book talent and re-license music for every adaptation. Buildable in the ElevenLabs UI in under 30 minutes; the kit carries the click-by-click guide.

Flows template B · the daily-use hook

Social cutdown

Takes one finished hero spot and returns three vertical cutdowns with market-correct captions and a re-voiced hook line per cut.

Most valuable for social and performance teams who receive a hero ad and need platform-ready variants the same day. They become the daily active users that make the account sticky.
