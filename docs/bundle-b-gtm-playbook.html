Page 1: Who to bring this to, and the math

Prospect profiles that convert on this story

1. Mid-market creative and media agencies (10 to 200 seats) with multi-country consumer accounts.
The teams quoting separate VO talent, studio time, and music licensing per market for every campaign adaptation. Pain: localization eats the margin on every regional rollout. The demo lands hardest with the head of production or an executive producer, not the creative director first.

2. In-house brand studios at consumer brands selling across Iberia and Western Europe.
Drinks (wine, beer, spirits, RTD), beauty, snacking, fashion, mobility: categories where the product is identical across markets but the moment of use is cultural. Pain: the home-market master ships in week one, the export versions ship in week five. Play them the same Cordoba film closing in every market's language; that is the whole pitch in twelve seconds.

3. Alcohol-category brands and their agencies specifically.
Alcohol advertising rules differ sharply by market (France's Loi Evin bans lifestyle and celebration framing; other markets restrict placement and claims). The demo set includes a French product-only cut produced from the same beats at zero additional generation cost. "Compliance variants are a re-edit, not a re-shoot" is a sentence this category has never heard.

4. Performance and social teams inside any of the above.
They need 9:16 cutdowns per market per platform, yesterday. The social cutdown template is their entry point; they become the daily active users that make the account sticky.

5. Localization and dubbing houses adapting ad inventory for EU broadcast and digital.
They already sell language services; this makes their unit economics dramatically better on short-form ad work. Position as capability expansion, not replacement.

Page 2: The first meeting, and the templates

Demo flow for a first meeting (12 minutes)

  1. Open with the reel (2.5 min). Play reel-one-script-seven-markets.mp4 full screen with sound. Say nothing until it ends. The point makes itself: same Cordoba film, same brand voice, and the language, the score, and the crowd's jerseys shift with the market.
  2. Show one localization decision (2 min). Pull up the ES and DE scripts side by side. Point at one line: "con la chispa justa" became "mit genau der richtigen Perlage." Perlage is how German wine copy actually talks about sparkle. This is transcreation, a creative act, not translation; their brand voice stays in charge.
  3. Show the compliance cut (1.5 min). Play the first seconds of spot-fr next to spot-fr-product-only. Same assets, one is shaped for Loi Evin. For any alcohol brand in the room this is the moment that matters.
  4. Open the flow live (3 min). Screen-share the Multi-market ad variant template. Change ONE thing in front of them (a market music prompt), rerun only that node, play the result. The rerun-one-node moment is the workflow argument in miniature.
  5. Talk money with receipts (1.5 min). Show the receipts table above. Every number traces to a public rate card or a logged API call.
  6. Close on their campaign (1.5 min). Ask which campaign is currently stuck in localization. Offer the 30 minute workshop with THEIR script on THEIR brand voice as the follow-up meeting.

What can go wrong in the room

  • Alcohol advertising rules: never improvise these. France (Loi Evin) restricts alcohol advertising to product-factual content in permitted placements; other markets have their own regimes. The workflow's answer is structural: compliance variants are cheap re-edits of approved assets, reviewed by the brand's counsel. Take the specific market question away and bring counsel-reviewed language back.
  • Voice rights: if the brand voice is cloned from a person, they need that person's consent and a talent agreement covering markets and media, like any VO buyout. Platform commercial-use rights follow the plan tier per the public pricing page.
  • "It sounds synthetic": play the PT or DE variant, not the one in the listener's own language first. Hearing one familiar voice move flawlessly between languages is the moment skeptics flip.
  • Sports imagery and alcohol: the demo set is compliance-tiered by market. Where match-night framing is permitted, the crowd wears plain kit in national colors only, no crests, no badges, no competition names (national colors are culture; federation marks are somebody's trademark). France and Italy restrict alcohol-and-sport association, so their cuts are a sport-free tier: same film, neutral summer wardrobe, summer-evening copy. France additionally gets the product-only cut: the strict product-factual shape Loi Evin requires in the channels where alcohol advertising is allowed at all (French TV bans it outright). Counsel reviews per market; this kit demonstrates workflow capability, not legal advice. Three tiers from one master is the whole workflow argument.
  • Brand safety on visuals: the demo brand is fictional on purpose. For a real brand, generated visuals run through the existing approval chain; the workflow feeds brand review faster, it does not bypass it.

Template descriptions (the two-sentence format)

Multi-market ad variant. Takes one master ad script plus transcreated copy per market and outputs a per-market voice track and market music bed in the same brand voice. Most valuable for brand and agency production teams running one campaign across several European markets who currently re-book talent and re-license music for every adaptation.

Social cutdown. Takes one finished hero spot and returns three vertical cutdowns with market-correct captions and a re-voiced hook line per cut. Most valuable for social and performance teams who receive a hero ad and need platform-ready variants the same day.

Success signals to log in deal notes

  • Prospect runs a template themselves inside 14 days (adoption, not just a demo).
  • Prospect brings a second team (social after brand, or legal asking about the compliance cut, which is a buying signal in the alcohol category).
  • Template output appears in a real campaign review deck. That artifact in their deck is the renewal argument.
