For account executives working media and publishing prospects. Two reusable Flows templates, one demo video, one workshop. Everything on this page is either measured in this bundle's run manifest or simple arithmetic on published list rates; there is nothing here you cannot defend on a call.

The two templates

Article to Audio Edition. Paste an article in, get a narrated, caption-verified audio edition out, in the publication's own consistent voice, localized into any market the outlet publishes in. Best for digital publishers and newsroom audience teams that want audio on every story without a narrator booking per story.

Brand Podcast Episode. An episode outline goes in; a scripted, voiced, sound-designed episode comes out of one Flows canvas. Best for brand content teams that want a recurring show, and for publishers piloting new formats before committing production budget.

The demo you can show

article-to-audience.mp4 (44 seconds): a 70-word metro brief becomes a branded audio edition, captions verified word-by-word against the audio by Speech to Text, then the same piece switches to Spanish with the read intact. Every API call is logged.

Scaled at list rates (TTS $0.10 per 1,000 characters, Scribe $0.22 per hour, dubbing $0.33 to $0.50 per minute, elevenlabs.io/pricing/api):

  • An 800-word feature narrates for about $0.48 and caption-verifies for under $0.02.
  • Each additional language on that feature runs $1.65 to $2.50 via dubbing.
  • A five-story daily briefing, two languages, prices at single-digit dollars per day.

Time, measured on the demo: narration in seconds, verification in seconds, the Spanish dub returned in under two minutes. The whole piece was produced inside one working session.

Do not quote studio rates, narrator day rates, or competitor pricing; we did not source them. Ask the prospect what an episode or an audio article costs them today and put their number next to this table.

Who to bring it to

  • Audience or product leads at digital-first outlets already publishing newsletters or briefings.
  • International or growth teams at outlets with multilingual readership.
  • Brand studios inside media companies that sell sponsored content and want audio inventory.

Never present any prospect as an ElevenLabs client. Reference public ElevenLabs media and publishing case studies from the official site only, and check the page is live before citing it.

The ask that works

Run the 30-minute workshop with their content team on one of THEIR articles. The template is adopted when their producers run it on their own stories without us in the room. Log template runs by the customer's own editors in your deal notes; that is the success metric this bundle is built around.
