Live answers · quick reference

The questions that will come up

"Whose voice is that?"
A designed voice from the library, or a voice the publication builds with consent and owns the use of. Cloning requires the speaker's explicit permission; that is policy, not preference.
"What does this cost at our volume?"
Use only the table in the AE one-pager and their own story counts. Do the arithmetic live on their numbers.
"Will it mispronounce our masthead / local names?"
Sometimes, on the first take. Show the fix live: adjust the text or spelling and re-run one node. Retakes are seconds and cents, and the STT check catches what you missed.
"Can this go in our CMS?"
The API covers everything the canvas did today, and run outputs export as standard files. Scope the integration conversation as the follow-up call; do not improvise architecture in the room.

Speaker notes · facilitator prep, 15 minutes, day beforeConfirm the customer workspace has ElevenCreative access and enough credits for roughly ten short generations; hover Run on any node to preview costs. Recreate Flows Template A in their workspace from the template guide (25 minutes the first time you ever do it; under 5 once you have your own saved copy to duplicate). Ask the customer to bring two published articles, 300 to 800 words, that they own the rights to. Never run text the customer does not own. Load the 44-second demo video locally as the backup demo in case of a live hiccup. Know their second market: which language would they localize into first? Pre-set the dub target. Success signals to log in deal notes: the template ran end to end on the customer's own article during the session; a named producer, not the AE, executed at least one run; the follow-up review call is booked with three of their stories committed; within two weeks, run history shows repeat template runs by customer staff on a paying workspace. That is the adoption metric this whole bundle reports against.
