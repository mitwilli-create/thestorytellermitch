The first workshops I'd put on Cursor's calendar

Here are the first three workshops I would put on Cursor's enterprise calendar, including who they serve and how we would know they worked.

  • Workshop pitch #1 · the economic-buyer briefing

    A session for the VP-Engineering and platform decision-makers who standardize an org on Cursor and defend that spend to a CFO. The productivity case, the security and admin answers, and what a rollout returns in the first quarter. This is the segment whose movement becomes pipeline, so it leads the calendar.

  • Workshop pitch #2 · the admin rollout clinic

    A session for the admins and engineering leaders running a Cursor deployment. Policy, enablement, and what the first 30 days of a rollout actually look like, built from the questions Sales and Customer Success field every week. I ran change communications for cross-org engineering mandates at Google. This is that muscle, pointed outward.

  • Workshop pitch #3 · the practitioner live-build

    A live-build hour for senior engineers. From tab-complete to agent workflows, on a real repo, no slideware. Hosted by someone who ships agentic systems in public, because this audience converts on demonstrated craft, not claims.

  • The machine behind the calendar

    A repeatable playbook. Topic intake from Sales, Customer Success, and Product. A quarterly theme arc. Speaker prep and a rehearsal for every session, run like a broadcast rundown. Follow-up out within 48 hours, while the room is still warm. Registration, reminders, and no-show recovery underneath. It is the same intake-and-governance model I built at Google, pointed at a shared pipeline view Sales plans around.

  • How we know it worked

    Pipeline first, the way the role is scored. A shared MQL definition with Sales, so qualified follow-up is what counts. An influenced-pipeline attribution model Sales trusts. Net-new accounts route to Sales, existing accounts route to Customer Success for expansion. Enterprise deals close on their own clock, so Day 90 is measured on leading indicators, not closed revenue. Then program quality, engagement, and community health. Reported monthly against a baseline built in the first 30 days.
