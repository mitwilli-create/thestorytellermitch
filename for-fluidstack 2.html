How I'd approach the Fluidstack narrative

Fluidstack has built one of the most consequential companies in AI and barely said so. Anthropic committed roughly $50 billion ↗ to build with Fluidstack as its deployment partner, and overnight a company most people couldn't name became the builder behind the biggest bet of the AI era. The exposure is the flip side: the public story is now so Anthropic-centered that reporters will test the concentration whether Fluidstack invites the question or not. The skeptical story will get written either way. The only question is who frames it first.

The approach is honesty as the strategy: bring the sourced version to the reporters before a critic does, keep every number labeled (committed spend is never revenue, and anything unconfirmed stays "reported" ↗ until a round closes), and turn Fluidstack's relentless milestone cadence into a deliberate rhythm. CoreWeave is the cautionary tale of what happens when critics frame the concentration story first. The positioning memo shows the full approach applied to Fluidstack.

The first 90 days, concretely

  • Pitch one · the build-out story

    The story nobody has written properly: what it takes to deliver Anthropic's $50 billion commitment, told from the gigawatt floor. That one goes to a tier-1 business desk plus one trade breakdown, sourced end to end. The committed-spend-is-never-revenue line is built in.

  • Pitch two · the financing explainer

    How Google's $1.73 billion backstop of Fluidstack's lease obligations ↗ actually works, walked through the filings, and why the circular-financing read misses the mechanics. That one lands on the capital-markets and AI-infrastructure beats before a short-seller frames it first.

  • The target map

    It starts where the network is strongest: the tier-1 business and AI desks. Then it builds the trade layer that covers Fluidstack weekly, and the analyst layer the role names: SemiAnalysis, Gartner, Forrester, DCD, and the capital-markets research desks that shape how the next round reads.

  • How coverage gets measured

    Share of voice against CoreWeave, Crusoe, Lambda, Nebius, and Hut 8. Coverage-tier quality over clip counts. Whether the sourced framing pulls through into the stories as written. Reported monthly.
