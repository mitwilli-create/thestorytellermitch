How I'd approach the Fluidstack narrative

Fluidstack has built one of the most consequential companies in AI and barely said so. Anthropic committed roughly $50 billion ↗ to build with it, and overnight a company most people couldn't name became the builder behind the biggest bet of the AI era. The exposure is the flip side: almost all of that business runs through one customer. The skeptical story will get written either way. The only question is who frames it first.

The approach is honesty as the strategy: bring the sourced version to the reporters before a critic does, keep every number labeled (committed spend is never revenue, and anything unconfirmed stays "reported" ↗ until a round closes), and turn Fluidstack's roughly one-milestone-a-quarter cadence into a deliberate rhythm. CoreWeave is the cautionary tale of what happens when critics frame the concentration story first. The positioning memo shows the full approach applied to Fluidstack.
