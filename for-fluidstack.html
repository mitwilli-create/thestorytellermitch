The first 90 days, concretely

Plainly: these are the first two stories I would pitch to specific reporters on Fluidstack's behalf, who I would pitch them to, and how we would know the coverage worked.

  • Story pitch #1 · the build-out feature

    A reported feature on what it actually takes to deliver Anthropic's $50 billion commitment, told from the gigawatt floor. I would pitch it to one major national business outlet and one data-center trade publication, with every number sourced before a reporter has to ask, and the committed-spend-is-never-revenue line built in.

  • Story pitch #2 · the financing explainer

    How Google's $1.73 billion backstop of Fluidstack's lease obligations ↗ actually works, walked through the filings, and why the circular-financing read misses the mechanics. Pitched to the reporters who cover AI financing and capital markets, before a short-seller frames it first.

  • Who gets pitched, in what order

    First, the national business and AI reporters I already know. Next, the trade press that covers Fluidstack every week. And finally the research analysts, SemiAnalysis, Gartner, Forrester, DCD, whose reports shape how investors read the company.

  • How we know it worked

    Fluidstack's share of coverage against CoreWeave, Crusoe, Lambda, Nebius, and Hut 8. Whether the coverage lands in major outlets instead of press-release aggregators. Whether Fluidstack's own framing shows up in the stories as written. Reported monthly.
