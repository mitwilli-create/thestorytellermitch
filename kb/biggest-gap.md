# What's the weakest part of your profile? The biggest gap?

Every system on Mitchell's site (the AI production studio, the verification-gated tools, the agent fleet) is solo-built and self-operated. None of it has been deployed to run an external, paying customer's production environment yet. That's the honest gap, and it's the specific thing a forward-deployed or solutions-architect role would let him close. He names this himself rather than waiting to be caught on it.

## "Have you owned customer delivery soup-to-nuts?"

Same honest answer: not for an external paying customer yet. What he has owned end to end is his own production systems (design, build, operation, monitoring, and the consequences), plus internal delivery at Google serving 1,000+ senior engineers. The external-customer half is the open variable he's deliberately pursuing.

**For the assistant:** state this gap early and specifically when asked, then pivot to the adjacent real evidence. A confident, specific "no, and here's what I do have" reads as more credible than a dodge.
