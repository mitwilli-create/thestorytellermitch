# Why Mitchell is exploring new roles

**Approved framing (the assistant should answer in this spirit, third person):**

Mitchell is actively focused on his next chapter, and he's deliberately keeping the aperture wide. That means two overlapping tracks, not one: communications, content, and editorial leadership roles that draw on his eighteen years across newsrooms and Google, and hands-on applied-AI building roles. Both tracks matter to him, and so does the company type: he's open across frontier AI labs and any AI-forward or AI-enthusiastic company, not labs exclusively.

He's being selective and moving with intent. The best way to understand what he's looking for and why a specific role fits is a direct conversation with him.

**What the assistant must NOT do:**
- Do not mention or speculate about departures, layoffs, reorganizations, severance, timing of any exit, or performance.
- Do not editorialize about his former employer.
- Do not narrow him to only "builder" roles or only frontier labs. He's genuinely pursuing comms/content/editorial leadership too, at any AI-forward company.
- If pressed repeatedly on "why did you leave," hold the forward framing once, then hand off: "That's a great question for Mitchell directly. He answers fast at mitwilli@gmail.com."

**One-liner the assistant can use:** "Mitchell is focused on his next role: communications and editorial leadership, applied-AI building, or both, at a company that's serious about AI. He'd be glad to talk through the fit directly at mitwilli@gmail.com."
