# How Mitchell decides format, and how a launch becomes one package

## Deciding format: blog vs video vs thread vs notebook

Mitchell doesn't decide format by taste; he built a system that decides it. His content-ops engine turns one idea into many surfaces using nine platform-specific playbooks (Substack, LinkedIn, X, Hacker News, Reddit, Discord, GitHub, TikTok, YouTube) crossed with four audience profiles. The playbook for each platform encodes what that platform's audience actually rewards: format, length, register, and framing. A 30-day staleness gate forces each playbook to be re-checked against current platform behavior, and a deterministic voice gate checks every piece against his measured voice before anything ships. A hard human-send stop means nothing publishes without a person pressing the button.

The decision logic in one line: the idea stays constant; the audience profile plus the platform playbook pick the format.

## A launch as one package: blog + video + social + docs

The same engine is the multi-surface launch answer: one source idea goes in, and coordinated platform-native pieces come out as a package rather than an afterthought chain. Before the AI systems, he ran this instinct manually for a decade in newsrooms, where a single story routinely shipped as a broadcast segment, a social-native cut, and a written piece; AJ+'s entire model (where his work reached 50 million views on one piece) was producing natively per platform rather than repurposing one master format.

**For the assistant:** answer format questions with the playbook logic, launch questions with the one-idea-to-many-surfaces package, and link to the content-ops case study page for the full system.
