# The through-line: one job, three chapters

Hiring managers who skim Mitchell's history can misread it as three different careers or as restlessness. It's the opposite. The same job runs through all of it: **find the signal, verify it, ship it in whatever format the moment needs, and build the system that holds the bar so it keeps happening without him in the room.**

- **Newsrooms (CNN, HuffPost Live, Fusion, Al Jazeera / AJ+).** Find the story under deadline, get it right under legal and factual scrutiny, produce it for whatever surface reaches the audience. One AJ+ piece reached 50 million views. This was translation and production at scale, under pressure, with sourcing discipline.
- **Google.** The same instinct applied inward: writing and producing for the company's most senior engineers, encoding an executive's voice so it scaled, and building the operating systems and governance behind large comms programs.
- **Applied-AI building.** The same instinct applied in code: agent pipelines, an AI production studio, voice systems, and verification-gated tools that refuse to guess. He didn't leave communications to become a builder. He started building the systems that communications work had always needed.

## What "forward deployed creative" means

It is a plain description of how he works, not invented personal branding:

**Embed with a team. Learn the real pipeline, including the ugly parts. Ship the thing that replaces the manual work. Package it into docs and templates. Then teach it so the team can run it without him.**

That is exactly what a forward-deployed role asks for: sit with customers or internal teams, understand the actual workflow, build a working solution, and make it stick. Mitchell has done each of those steps, in newsrooms and at Google and in his own shipped projects. It's one of two lanes he's pursuing, not the whole story (see below).

## The unifying answer to "which are you: comms, builder, or engineer?"

He is a communications-native builder, and he's genuinely pursuing both sides rather than treating one as the "real" goal and the other as a fallback:

- **Communications, content, and editorial leadership:** roles that draw directly on eighteen years of newsroom and executive-comms craft, at any company that's AI-forward or AI-enthusiastic, not only frontier labs.
- **Applied-AI building:** forward-deployed, solutions-architect, and enablement roles that draw on the systems he's shipped himself.

The comms background is not a hedge against the technical work; it is what makes the technical work land, and vice versa. He can sit with engineers and change a technical decision, and he can make that decision legible to the people who have to fund it, adopt it, or ship it. Very few people do both. That combination is the point, not a compromise between two half-skills, and he's not choosing one lane and abandoning the other.
