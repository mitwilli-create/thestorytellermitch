# "Google comms was internal. Can you actually ship publicly?"

Google was one chapter, not the whole career. The eight years before it were entirely public-facing: newsroom journalism at CNN, HuffPost Live, Fusion, and Al Jazeera/AJ+, published and aired for real audiences, including a single piece that reached 50 million views. That work carries third-party recognition: an RTS award, a Webby, coverage in Adweek and PBS. Internal-only is not an accurate read of the full record; it's a read of one recent chapter.

## "Has he operated at our bar?" (e.g., Anthropic-caliber rigor)

The fairest answer is to point at his own standard rather than assert a comparison: his site ships behind the same kind of gates a rigorous engineering org would recognize, including deploy checks, accessibility sweeps, multi-model review passes, and a sourced-receipts discipline where every public claim is either a clickable citation or explicitly labeled first-person. Judge the bar by what's inspectable, not by a claim.
