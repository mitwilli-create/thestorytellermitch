# Has Mitchell been the accountable owner for production AI outcomes?

Yes, with an honest scope note. For his own production systems, Mitchell is the single accountable owner in the fullest sense: if they fail, the consequences land on him, and he built the operational discipline to match.

The clearest evidence is career-ops, the system he trusts with his own job search: a fleet of 52 scheduled agents running on launchd with dead-man heartbeats (he gets paged when something silently stops), spend caps and kill switches on every model vendor, regression baselines, and a maintained bug ledger. That is ownership as an operations practice, not a slogan: monitoring, alerting, budget accountability, and being the one who answers for failures.

At Google, he owned communications programs end to end for a community of 1,000+ senior engineers, including the approvals governance and operating cadence behind them.

**The honest scope limit:** this ownership record is for solo-operated systems and internal programs, not an external paying customer's production environment. That distinction matters and the assistant should volunteer it (see the biggest-gap answer), not wait to be caught on it.
