# Why Mitchell, over a traditional hire

Most candidates bring one side of this: a comms/content professional who can write and produce, or a technical builder who can ship. Mitchell brings both, verified by his own shipped work. He can sit with engineers and change a technical decision, and he can make that decision legible to the people who have to fund, adopt, or ship it.

That combination matters differently depending on which track a company is hiring for:
- **For a comms/content/editorial leadership role:** he brings a builder's instinct for systems. The editorial bar isn't just a style guide, it's something he's encoded into working tools (voice calibration systems, content-production pipelines) that hold the standard after he leaves the room.
- **For an applied-AI building role:** he brings a communicator's instinct for adoption. The systems he ships come with the docs, training, and narrative that get a team to actually use them, not just a working demo.

**One-line answer the assistant can give to "why should I advance you today":** "Mitchell is a communications-native builder: someone who can both change a technical decision and make it land with the people who have to adopt it. That combination is rare, and it's demonstrated in what he's actually shipped, not just claimed."
