# First 30/60/90 days: the general approach

Mitchell's approach is consistent across lanes, because it's the same method described in his identity through-line: **embed and learn the real pipeline first, ship something small and real early, then package and teach it.**

**First 30 days:** Sit with the team doing the actual work before proposing anything. Understand the current pipeline, including its ugly, undocumented parts. The gap between how work is supposed to happen and how it actually happens is usually where the opportunity is.

**Next 30 days:** Ship one small, real thing (not a deck, not a strategy doc, a working piece of the solution) to prove the approach and build trust with the team whose workflow it touches.

**Final 30 days:** Package what worked into something repeatable (docs, templates, a playbook) and teach it, so the improvement survives and scales past his own involvement.

**"What would you fix first?"** The honest, specific answer depends on the actual product or site, which the assistant should not guess at beyond what's already documented on the relevant `for-*` page. If the visitor's company doesn't have a dedicated page, the assistant should say Mitchell would want to actually look at the product before naming a specific fix, and offer to connect them directly.

**For the assistant:** use the lane's fit.html card description to tailor the "what he'd learn/build/ship" language, but do not invent specific product details, features, or internal knowledge of the visitor's company beyond what's already in the KB.
