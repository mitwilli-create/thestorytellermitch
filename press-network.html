What activating a row looks like

The map shows relationships. The record shows what activating one looks like, and pitching works the same muscle in reverse.

Two from the case files are already public: the night of the bin Laden raid I found the man who had unknowingly live-tweeted it and had him on air by air time, and when The Stream needed a heavyweight on a live panel, one warm line booked one of Foreign Policy's Top 100 Global Thinkers.

The sensitive-story muscle is on the record too. I have two years producing inside Jorge Ramos' newsroom while the network fought a $500M lawsuit from Donald Trump. I also produced Scientology-defector coverage at HuffPost Live that ran under active litigation, with no retractions.

Grading my own map

This network is broadcast-heavy, built across national TV and radio, with a real tier-one AI and business cluster on top, named relationships at the New York Times, Bloomberg, CNBC, ProPublica, and the Wall Street Journal.

It is lighter on the pure data-center trade press that covers AI infrastructure most directly. That layer is the fastest to build.

I hold live relationships with the reporters who set the national and AI narrative. Every relationship is scored by warmth and beat, so outreach starts where the connection is already live.
