How this site is made and checked

Systems and checks.

This site ships through the discipline it argues for. Every change passes a fixed set of gates before deploy, each gate is a script, and the record below is what the gates measured: dated, with flags listed while they were open and marked when they closed.

The standing gates, every deploy

  • Definition auditA headless browser walks every page at two viewports and compares each rendered image, poster, and loop against its intrinsic pixels. Anything under 1.6x its rendered size gets re-sourced from a better master or logged as a known ceiling.
  • Bake driftGenerated regions (the reel, the case files, the writing index) must match their generators exactly. Hand edits inside baked markup fail the build.
  • Loop seamsEvery ambient loop is a measured self-crossfade: seam scored at the cycle boundary, palette checked against the approved still, motion compared to the accepted predecessor, and a frame montage reviewed by eye before anything ships.
  • LoudnessAudio-bearing clips are measured to EBU R128 and normalized to -16 LUFS with true peaks held at -1.5 dBTP.
  • PerformanceLargest contentful paint under 2.5 seconds and zero layout shift, held at 1440 and 390 wide, measured under a throttled harness and again on the live site after deploy.
  • AccessibilityKeyboard, touch, reduced-motion, and focus behavior are exercised on every playable surface, not assumed from markup.
  • Code reviewEvery commit set goes through automated review and doesn't ship until majors and regressions read zero.
  • Outside eyesEach shipped round of changes is reviewed by a seven-model panel, and its verdict is logged before the next round starts.
  • Edge freshnessAfter deploy, both hosts are polled until the new bytes actually serve. The CDN's word isn't taken for it.

The same gates, deployed at a customer

A forward-deployed engagement doesn't end when the pipeline works; it ends when the customer's team runs the pipeline and the quality holds without the builder in the room. That's what these gates are for. Each one maps to its analog in a customer deployment.

  • Definition audit → asset mastersEvery deliverable serves at the definition the customer's surfaces need: the same headless walk, run against their CDN masters instead of mine.
  • Bake drift → template governanceGenerated campaign variants must match the approved master template exactly. Hand edits inside generated regions fail the build there for the same reason they fail it here.
  • Loudness → per-brand audio specThe -16 LUFS target becomes the brand's own loudness spec, held across every deliverable and every dub language, measured, not eyeballed.
  • Loop seams → publish gates for motion assetsAny looping brand asset gets its boundary measured before it ships, so nothing on a customer's homepage visibly jumps.
  • Performance → campaign page budgetsThe same largest-contentful-paint and layout-shift ceilings, held on the customer's stack at desktop and mobile widths, measured under throttle and again on the live property after launch.
  • Accessibility → dated test evidenceKeyboard, touch, and reduced-motion paths exercised on every branded playable surface, with a dated record of what was tested and what it showed, the raw material an accessibility review starts from.
  • Code review + outside eyes → creative review boardTemplates and configs reviewed to zero majors before handoff; each campaign round gets an independent review pass before publish, the pattern this site runs with a seven-model panel.
  • Edge freshness → launch verificationThe campaign is live when the customer's edge actually serves the new bytes, not when the deploy said so.

Worked example, localization. A marketing team localizes one hero campaign across three languages through the Dubbing API, the same stage the broll pipeline runs for its Spanish and Japanese cuts. The loudness gate becomes the per-language LUFS check across dub tracks. The definition audit covers each localized cut's masters. Bake drift keeps every regional variant pinned to the approved master. Outside eyes adds a native-speaker pass per market. Edge freshness confirms each region serves the new cut before anyone announces it. Day one of the engagement is building that workflow; day thirty is these gates running on their own, which is why the operator starter kit is written for the customer's producer, not for me.

The interaction contract, published

Every playable tile on this site holds the same contract, exercised rather than assumed: 153 tiles across 5 pages in the last sweep, 114 of 114 hover previews muted, two bugs found and fixed the same day.

  • HoverA muted preview plays inside the tile's own bounds, showing the same clip the thumbnail comes from. Desktop sugar only; it carries no meaning the tile doesn't.
  • Click and tapOne action opens the in-page theater with visible controls. No two-step preview grammar on touch; the first tap plays the artifact.
  • KeyboardEnter and Space open the same theater; Escape closes it and focus returns to the tile that opened it; focus stays inside the theater while it's open.
  • Reduced motionPreviews hold their poster frame. Nothing autoplays for a viewer who asked it not to.
  • Screen readersTiles carry labels naming the piece and the role; decorative previews are hidden from assistive tech.
  • CaptionsThe produced short ships with English captions and dubbed Spanish and Japanese cuts with caption tracks; archive clips carry title, role, and duration metadata on every tile.
