2007 · 2008

WDRB Fox 41
Louisville

Overnight Producer · Fox in the Morning

The origin: the overnight shift, owning two hours of the four-hour live morning block. When the Bhutto assassination broke during my window, we beat the networks to air from Kentucky. The template for every rebuilt rundown since.

the backdrop

WDRB Fox 41, Louisville, Kentucky's Fox affiliate. A small-market local station, where morning news runs on long live blocks with tiny crews. I produced overnights there from September 2007 to May 2008. Real deadlines, no safety net: that block is where live television started for me.

2010 · 2011

Al Jazeera English
The Stream

Founding-team Associate Producer

Launch night, May 2, 2011: the planned rundown abandoned when the bin Laden raid broke, and the man who unknowingly live-tweeted it booked on air, his follower count climbing from a few hundred to tens of thousands. Social-data journalism before it was a category.

the backdrop

Al Jazeera English launched The Stream in May 2011. Bold bet: social media itself was the story engine, a daily news show built around live audience input, not a fixed script. Within a year it held a Royal Television Society award for innovative news. I joined the founding production team in 2010, building a format nobody had run before.

March 2, 2011

The moment that
opened the door

On March 2, 2011, Secretary of State Hillary Clinton told the Senate Foreign Relations Committee: "So we are in an information war. And we are losing that war... Al-Jazeera is winning. The Chinese have opened up a global English language and multilanguage television network... We are cutting back." Delivered two months before The Stream's launch night, the line named exactly what newsrooms like mine were building. It was a starting gun: within a year, competing broadcasters were staffing up, hiring producers with the live, social-first skills The Stream had spent 2011 sharpening.

2011 · 2012

CGTN America

Line Producer · daily rundown + Saturday news hour · DC bureau

CGTN America's launch year. I owned the daily rundown at the brand-new DC bureau, line produced and boothed the Saturday weekend news hour anchored by Elaine Reyes, and segment produced for the reporters building Biz Asia. Live-broadcast discipline carried straight from The Stream into a newsroom assembled from zero. In August 2012, I took the same operating rhythm to HuffPost Live.

the job, hour by hour

Building the rundown for a daily one-hour live news program. Sequencing segments. Coordinating talent, graphics, and field correspondents across time zones. Re-cutting the hour when news broke inside the broadcast window. Saturdays added the booth: calling the live weekend hour as it aired. A US bureau of an internationally funded broadcaster also meant navigating editorial standards across funder, audience, and source markets.

HuffPost Live launched August 13, 2012. Roy Sekoff, founding editor. The bet: eight hours of live daily streaming, built around viewer participation, guests joining by Skype and Google+ Hangouts. A news network years before streaming news was normal television.

2013 · 2015

Fusion

Senior Producer · America With Jorge Ramos

Weekly primetime on the ABC/Univision joint venture, produced end to end: full episodes, election specials, field segments, and the 44-minute Mandela special, live on a rundown rebuilt as the news broke.

the backdrop

Fusion launched October 28, 2013, a joint venture between ABC and Univision aimed at English-speaking millennials, including Latino millennials, mixing news with satire and pop culture. Its primetime flagship was America with Jorge Ramos, which brought the veteran Noticiero Univision anchor's immigration, gun control, and civil rights interviewing into English for the first time.

The growth was public record: by Fusion's two-year mark, total-day viewership was up 183% (primetime, 179%), a top-10 launch among historical cable networks, the audience over-indexing +85% in Hispanic households and +48% in African American households. Network-level numbers, on the record in the trade press during the window ↗.

2016 · 2018

AJ+

Senior Producer

Viral social news while AJ+ was defining the category. I led a team of 10+ producers during the years the network reached 500 million weekly viewers, field-produced the San Juan mayor's interview during active Hurricane Maria conditions, and designed the day-of explainer production line whose proof-of-concept hit 50 million views.

the backdrop

AJ+ launched September 15, 2014, built out of the former Current TV headquarters in San Francisco that Al Jazeera had just acquired, a mobile-first, digital-native offshoot aimed at millennials who had never watched a cable newscast. It went on to define the captioned, short-form social video format most news publishers later copied, becoming one of the most-viewed news publishers on Facebook.
