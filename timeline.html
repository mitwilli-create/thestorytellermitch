2007 · 2008

WDRB Fox 41
Louisville

Overnight Producer · Fox in the Morning

The origin: owning two hours of a live morning block at a small-market station. When the Bhutto assassination broke during my overnight window, we beat the networks to air from Kentucky. The template for every rebuilt rundown since.

what this was

WDRB Fox 41, Louisville, Kentucky's Fox affiliate. A small-market local station. Morning news runs on long live blocks with tiny crews. From September 2007 to May 2008, I was the overnight producer, owning two hours of the four-hour live morning block. Real deadlines, no safety net, that block is where live television started for me.

2010 · 2011

Al Jazeera English
The Stream

Founding-team Associate Producer

Launch night, May 2, 2011: the planned rundown abandoned when the bin Laden raid broke, and the man who unknowingly live-tweeted it booked on air, his follower count climbing from a few hundred to tens of thousands. Social-data journalism before that was a category.

what this was

Al Jazeera English launched The Stream in May 2011. Bold bet: social media itself was the story engine. A daily news show built around live audience input, not a fixed script. First broadcast night, the planned rundown collided with the bin Laden raid. The show booked the man who had unknowingly live-tweeted it. Within a year, a Royal Television Society award for innovative news. I was on the founding production team, building a format nobody had run before.

March 2, 2011

The moment that
opened the door

On March 2, 2011, Secretary of State Hillary Clinton told the Senate Foreign Relations Committee: "So we are in an information war. And we are losing that war... Al-Jazeera is winning. The Chinese have opened up a global English language and multilanguage television network... We are cutting back." The line named what newsrooms like mine had been building, and it landed as a starting gun. Within a year, competing broadcasters were staffing up fast, hiring producers with the live, social-first skills The Stream had spent that year sharpening.

2011 · 2012

CCTV America

Line Producer · owned the daily rundown · DC bureau

CCTV's American launch year. I owned the daily rundown at the brand-new DC bureau, carrying the live-broadcast discipline from The Stream into a newsroom being assembled from zero. That August, I took the same operating rhythm to HuffPost Live.

what this was

CCTV America was China Central Television's American production hub. It launched from Washington on February 6, 2012, anchored by Mike Walter and Elaine Reyes, hiring dozens of journalists from NBC, Bloomberg, Fox, CNN, BBC, and ABC for its new DC bureau. HuffPost Live

Segment Producer

Streaming-first live news before streaming-first was normal. I originated and produced panels years ahead of their policy fights: PrEP six months after FDA approval, transgender service members before the Pentagon moved.

HuffPost Live launched August 13, 2012. Roy Sekoff, founding editor. The bet: eight hours of live daily streaming, built around viewer participation, guests joining by Skype and Google+ Hangouts. A news network years before streaming news was normal television.

Fusion

Senior Producer · America With Jorge Ramos

Weekly primetime on the ABC/Univision joint venture, produced end to end: full episodes, election specials, field segments, and the 44-minute live special on Mandela's death built on a rebuilt rundown.

what this was

Fusion launched October 28, 2013, a joint venture between ABC and Univision aimed at English-speaking millennials, including Latino millennials, mixing news with satire and pop culture. Its primetime flagship was America with Jorge Ramos, which brought the veteran Noticiero Univision anchor's immigration, gun control, and civil rights interviewing into English for the first time.

2016 · 2018

AJ+

Senior Producer

Viral social news at the top of its category-defining era. I ran a team during the years AJ+ reached 500 million weekly viewers, field-produced San Juan's mayor during active Hurricane Maria conditions, and made one piece that hit 50 million views on its own.

what this was

AJ+ launched September 15, 2014, built out of the former Current TV headquarters in San Francisco that Al Jazeera had just acquired, a mobile-first, digital-native offshoot aimed at millennials who had never watched a cable newscast. It went on to define the captioned, short-form social video format most news publishers later copied, becoming one of the most-viewed news publishers on Facebook.
